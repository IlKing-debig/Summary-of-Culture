China


Overview

China is a country in East Asia and is the world's most populous nation (alongside India). It is a global power with one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, spanning thousands of years.


Geography

  • Size: It is the third or fourth largest country by total area, featuring diverse landscapes from the Gobi and Taklamakan Deserts in the north to subtropical forests in the south.

  • Mountains: It contains the Himalayas to the west, including Mount Everest on the border with Nepal.

  • Rivers: The Yangtze (the longest in Asia) and the Yellow River (the "cradle of Chinese civilization") are its most vital waterways.

History

  • Ancient Dynasties: China's history is characterized by a succession of dynasties (such as the Qin, Han, Tang, Ming, and Qing). The Great Wall of China was built over centuries to protect these empires.

  • Imperial Collapse: The dynastic system ended in 1912 with the Xinhai Revolution, leading to the Republic of China.

  • People's Republic: Following a civil war, the Communist Party of China, led by Mao Zedong, established the People's Republic of China (PRC) in 1949.

Politics and Government

  • System: China is a unitary one-party socialist republic led by the Communist Party of China (CPC).

  • Administrative Divisions: It consists of 22 provinces (considering Taiwan as the 23rd), five autonomous regions, four direct-controlled municipalities (Beijing, Tianjin, Shanghai, Chongqing), and two special administrative regions (Hong Kong and Macau).

Economy

  • Global Powerhouse: Since economic reforms in 1978, China has become the world's second-largest economy by nominal GDP and the largest by PPP (Purchasing Power Parity).

  • Industry: It is the world's largest manufacturer and exporter, often referred to as "the world's factory."

  • Technology: China has become a leader in high-tech sectors, including 5G, artificial intelligence, and high-speed rail.

Culture and Society

  • Language: Standard Mandarin is the official language, though many regional dialects (like Cantonese) exist.

  • Philosophy: Traditional Chinese values are heavily influenced by Confucianism, Taoism, and Buddhism.

  • Innovation: Ancient China is famous for the "Four Great Inventions": the compass, gunpowder, papermaking, and printing.
